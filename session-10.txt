Types of DNS records
=====================
NS  -> Nameservers, who is managing the domain
SOA -> Start of authority
by using NS & SOA we can transfer domain authority to anyone

A -> IPv4
AAAA -> IPv6
MX -> Mail records
gmail -- give free for public
gmail -- paid for business
if we want a mail with our domain(@sowjanya.com) name we approach gmail and they will charge for that and we have to update mx(mailx) records for that in our domain
for ex we have to update smtp.google.com

TXT -> for Verification (they will give content we have to update it in domain , they will try to access our domain , if they find the given value -- our domain verification become success -- during ssl renewal)
CNAME	-> Canonical name, instead of ip address (in value) we can give another domain name

nslookup checks for A record bydefault

command : nslookup -type=CNAME <domain-name>
it gives another domain name specified in cname
